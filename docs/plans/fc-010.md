## Context

Call stop-losses cost $20,391 in the last 60 days while all profitable closes earned only $5,882 — a net loss of $14,509 from the stop-loss mechanism alone. When a covered call goes deep ITM (stock rallies above strike), the stop-loss pays $900-$2,095 in real cash to buy back the call. But assignment on a covered call is profitable by construction: the cost-basis protection filter guarantees strike > cost basis, so assignment is always a gain.

The "loss" from assignment is foregone upside (opportunity cost), not cash out of pocket. With FC-006 (rolling engine) now live, the "stock rallied through my strike" scenario is handled by economics-aware Friday rolling, making the panic-buyback stop-loss redundant and harmful.

Data from BQ analysis (60-day window):
- 65 profitable closes: +$5,882 total (avg +$90.50)
- 13 stop-loss closes: -$20,391 total (avg -$1,568.54)
- GOOGL260415C00312500: 10 duplicate stop-loss events losing $17,590 on a $493.50 premium position

## Goals

- Eliminate cash-destructive call stop-loss buybacks
- Let covered calls expire or get assigned naturally (profitable by design)
- Rely on FC-006 rolling engine for ITM call management

## Non-goals

- Changing put stop-loss behavior (already disabled, correct for wheel strategy)
- Removing stop-loss code (keep it available as a config toggle for future use)

## Proposed approach

Single config change: `config/settings.yaml` → `use_call_stop_loss: true` → `false`

No code changes needed — the stop-loss logic is already gated by this config flag in `call_seller.should_close_call_early()`.

## Critical files

- `config/settings.yaml` — `use_call_stop_loss: false`

## Risks & mitigations

- **Covered call goes deep ITM, shares called away below market price**
  - Mitigation: This is profitable by design (strike > cost basis). FC-006 handles rolling to higher strikes on Fridays. Assignment means you sold shares at a price you agreed to.

- **Extreme ITM scenario (stock doubles)**
  - Mitigation: Foregone upside is the known cost of covered call strategy. The alternative (stop-loss buyback) costs real cash and has proven to lose 3.5x more than it saves.
